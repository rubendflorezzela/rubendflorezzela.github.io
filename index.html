About me
Building AI that understands
the human mind in real time

I build multimodal AI systems that monitor human cognitive state in real time — with a focus on safety-critical environments where cognitive impairment means lives at risk.

My work integrates EEG signal processing, computer vision, and deep learning to design interpretable, deployable architectures that go beyond lab prototypes: systems that run on embedded hardware, handle real-world noise, and produce outputs that can be trusted in high-stakes decisions.

I am a researcher at the LIECAR Laboratory and lecturer at the Universidad Nacional de San Antonio Abad del Cusco (UNSAAC), Peru, recognized as a RENACYT Level V researcher by CONCYTEC. I serve as a peer reviewer for international scientific publications including Scientific Reports (Nature Portfolio) and ACM Transactions on Intelligent Systems and Technology.

Focus Areas
Research Interests

Cognitive State Monitoring

Real-time detection of drowsiness, fatigue, and mental workload in operators of safety-critical systems such as vehicles, aviation, and industrial control.

EEG Signal Processing

Biomarker extraction, artifact removal, and feature engineering from EEG signals for reliable cognitive state classification under real-world conditions.

Multimodal AI Fusion

Combining EEG, eye-tracking (RGB–NIR cameras), and facial landmark dynamics for robust, redundant cognitive state inference beyond single-modality limits.

Embedded Deep Learning

Deploying CNN and hybrid models on edge platforms (NVIDIA Jetson Nano) for low-latency, on-device inference in real-world applications.

Interpretable AI for Human Factors

Moving beyond black-box models toward systems that are auditable, explainable, and reliable — a critical requirement for high-stakes AI deployment.

Active project
Current Research
Active · Targeting Q1 submission 2026

Multimodal Neuro-Ocular System for
Driver Drowsiness Detection

The leading cause of fatal road accidents is not mechanical failure — it's cognitive impairment behind the wheel. This project develops a real-time monitoring system that detects driver drowsiness and mental fatigue by fusing EEG biomarkers, eye-tracking dynamics via RGB and NIR cameras, and facial landmark modeling.

Signals are processed through a multimodal deep learning architecture and deployed on an NVIDIA Jetson Nano for real-time, on-vehicle inference. A key focus is interpretability — using attention mechanisms and saliency maps to make model decisions transparent and trustworthy for safety-critical deployment.
